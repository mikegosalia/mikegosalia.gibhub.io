The Drug from Mumbai

A thrilling romantic-suspense novel

"When I opened "The Drug from Mumbai" and realized the author was writing as Timothy, a senior in college, I was fascinated to read how adroitly he was tricked and seamlessly captivated into becoming a drug courier. I was smitten by Timothy's audacity, naivete, and willingness to seek adventure. I had to follow him to Paris and Mumbai to make sure he would succeed and stay alive. It took me two days to read this book. I loved this character; now I'm waiting impatiently for a sequel. Hurry up, Mike!"

"A fast pace read with fun characters. There's action, adventure, romance and never a dull moment. My favorite characters were the quirky Mr. Abdul and the spunky love interest Alexis."

Writing samples:

Prologue, pg 3.


 The starting gun had been fired. I could see all the guys chasing me, and so I was off. Down the hall I went. I barreled into a set of double doors. They flew open. I raced outside onto the school grounds, running as hard as I could. Forget my backpack. I took it off long before I ever hit the grass.
 Oh, God, I thought, Why’d I ever get myself into this mess? Jealousy? Selfishness? What was it? I’d been in love with Deborah for so long now that when I saw Cody cheating on her at a party with her best friend, I couldn’t help but say something. And now she knows I’m in love with her!
 Just then I jumped a bench that was sitting there on the grass next to the soccer field and continued on my way, racing as hard as I could down the track. Cody and his henchmen were nigh on my heels. I had absolutely no time to turn around and check where they were. Hell, I knew they were there. Finally I reached the track. I zipped through the gated entrance and ran onto one of the lanes. Down it I sped. The others followed.

Chapter 7, pg 94.


 “Well, listen,” she continued, “I overheard you talking on the phone the other day, when you were at my party, and I know that you have something going on tomorrow.”
 “Wait, what?”
 “Yeah, a delivery.”
 I looked back at Alexis, astonished.
 “So what if I do?”
 “Well, I want you to take me along. If you say no I’ll tell your friend here. You wouldn’t want that, now would you?”
 I paused for a moment. I surely wouldn’t want that, but I also remembered what Susan told me, that I shouldn’t tell anyone about this.
 “Now, you listen here,” I said, reaching across the table to catch hold of Alexis’s arm. “This isn’t a joke. You can’t just do this to me.”
 She just laughed.
 “Yes, I can,” she said.
